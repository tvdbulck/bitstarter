FAQ

You mention that this is a crowdfunding project. What is crowdfunding?
Crowdfunding is a relatively new way of funding initiatives of individual people or organizations through the joint contributions of many people (in our case: you!). It can be used to start a new company. See also this Wikipedia article.
Do you really make unique jewelry or is this just a sly way to attract customers?
All our jewels are guaranteed unique, we will never create the same jewel twice. Every jewel is separately 3d printed and uniquely tailored to you, either based on your DNA or on your personal profile. When ordering a DNA-based jewel, you receive a certificate of uniqueness.
I don't like to be DNA-tested, can I still order a jewel?
Yes. Jewelry can be uniquified in two ways: through a DNA fingerprint or through your personal profile. If you prefer not to do the DNA test, just create a personal profile and we create the unique 'pDNA' (=personal DNA) fingerprint for you from this profile.
What is DNA fingerprinting?
DNA fingerprinting is used in forensic analysis to uniquely identify an individual. It is usually used in parental testing or for criminal investigations (yes, CSI style!). See also this Wikipedia article.
Who conducts the DNA fingerprinting tests?

We collaborate with a professional lab whose core business is DNA fingerprinting. Your DNA samples are submitted directly to this lab.
How is the DNA test done? Will it hurt?
Nope, DNA testing does not hurt! No needles, no pain, just some chewing gum ;-). You will receive a kit with chewing gum: just chew a gum for a few minutes and send the gum back to us. How simple is that?!
What will you do with my DNA results?
Nothing. We have no interest in DNA results whatsoever, so normally DNA fingerprinting results are not stored anywhere. However, if you would like to order additional DNA-based jewelry in the future, it might be interesting to still have the fingerprinting results available. This will save you a (costly) additional DNA test. Bottom line: it's up to you to decide if you want to keep the results for future reference or not.
I would like to surprise my partner with a unique piece of jewelry. Can I do the DNA test without my partner knowing this?
Sorry, for obvious reasons of privacy protection, this is not allowed. Fortunately, you can surprise your partner by generating a 'pDNA' based on their personal profile.
